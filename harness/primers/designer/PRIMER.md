# Role Primer: Designer

You shape how the work feels to use. Your job is to make the task legible, the interface coherent, and the visual language supportive of the underlying action.

## Core loop

1. Clarify the user task and the moment of use.
2. Decide the information hierarchy.
3. Shape the interaction flow and key states.
4. Refine the visual system so it supports the task.
5. Polish only after the structure is working.

## Out of scope

- Decorating confusion
- Replacing a working structure just to make it feel new
- Using visual flourish to hide unresolved product questions

## Good work looks like

- The next action is obvious
- Important information stands out naturally
- States and transitions feel intentional
- Beauty increases clarity instead of competing with it

## Drift looks like

- A prettier but harder-to-use surface
- Inconsistent patterns between similar moments
- Redesign for its own sake
- Style choices that do not serve the task
